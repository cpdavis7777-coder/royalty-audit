\# Royalty Audit Tool (working name: Derrick)



\## Problem

12+ million Americans own mineral rights and receive royalty checks from oil and gas operators. Most inherited them, are non-technical, and have no way to verify the checks are accurate. Operators underpay by 5-20% routinely, through accounting errors or deliberate deductions. The incumbent audit market is $1,500+ specialized auditors. No self-serve consumer tool exists.



\## Target User

\- Primary: Individual mineral rights owners (ages 45-75), receiving monthly royalty checks between $100 and $10,000, with no technical or legal background.

\- Secondary: Estate attorneys and CPAs who manage mineral interests for clients.

\- Tertiary: Heirs who have inherited mineral rights and have no idea what they actually own.



\## Value Proposition

Upload your royalty check. We compare it against public state production data and market pricing to tell you, in plain English, whether you are being paid what you are owed. $49 per audit or $19/month for ongoing tracking.



\## Core User Flow (v1)

1\. User lands on site, sees clear explanation and sample output.

2\. User uploads a PDF or image of their royalty check stub.

3\. System extracts key fields using Claude Vision.

4\. System fetches public production data from Texas Railroad Commission (Texas only for v1).

5\. System fetches commodity price data from EIA for the relevant month.

6\. System calculates expected royalty and compares to actual.

7\. System generates a plain-English audit report showing variance, likely explanations, and next steps.

8\. User can download the report as a PDF.

\## Constraints and Disclaimers

\- Tool is informational only, not legal or financial advice

\- State data sometimes lags by 30-90 days; disclosed in UI

\- Deductions (transportation, processing, compression) vary by lease and are estimated using industry averages in v1

\- v1 handles oil and natural gas only, not NGL or condensate edge cases
